GETTING HERE

Reaching Vancouver Island is a breeze. Easily accessible by air and sea, the island welcomes visitors via commercial flights, ferries, and private boats. Whether you're flying into one of its airports, enjoying a scenic ferry ride from the mainland, or arriving in your own vessel, getting here is part of the adventure. Vancouver Island's diverse transportation options ensure a smooth start to your journey in this Pacific paradise.

BY AIR

Vancouver Island is easily accessible by air. The island has three major airports, located in Victoria, Nanaimo, and Comox. Each airport offers a variety of services, including car rentals, taxis, and shuttles. If you're flying into Vancouver International Airport (YVR), you can take a short flight to one of the island's airports. Or, you can take a scenic ferry ride from the mainland to Vancouver Island.

BY LAND

Vancouver Island is easily accessible by land. The island is connected to the mainland by two major ferry routes, and you can also reach it via the United States. If you're driving to Vancouver Island, you can take a scenic ferry ride from the mainland. Or, you can drive through the United States and cross the border into Canada. Once you're on the island, you can explore its diverse regions by car, bus, or train.
